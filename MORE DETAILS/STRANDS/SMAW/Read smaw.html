What is Smaw Strand?

SMAW stands for "Shielded Metal Arc Welding," and it typically refers to a specific skill or specialization within the Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) strand. In the context of education or vocational training, SMAW focuses on teaching students the techniques and principles of shielded metal arc welding. Students in the SMAW strand would learn how to use manual welding equipment, understand welding processes, and acquire the necessary skills to join metal pieces using shielded metal arc welding techniques. This type of vocational training prepares individuals for careers in welding and related industries where expertise in SMAW is essential.

Why SMAW

The SMAW (Shielded Metal Arc Welding) strand within the TVL track focuses on welding skills and techniques. By choosing SMAW, students can develop expertise in welding processes commonly used in various industries. Opportunities include careers as welders, metal fabricators, and construction workers. The course typically covers welding theory, safety practices, and hands-on training in SMAW techniques. Graduates may find employment in construction, manufacturing, shipbuilding, and other industries where welding is a crucial skill. Additionally, they can explore further certifications or apprenticeships to advance their welding career..
