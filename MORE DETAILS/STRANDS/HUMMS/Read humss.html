What is HUMSS Strand?

HUMSS stands for "Humanities and Social Sciences." It is a specialized track or program in the senior high school level that focuses on subjects related to social sciences, humanities, and other related fields. The HUMSS strand is designed to provide students with a broad understanding of human behavior, society, culture, and various social issues. Students who choose the HUMSS strand often have interests in fields such as psychology, sociology, anthropology, political science, and literature. The curriculum aims to develop critical thinking, communication, and research skills.

Why HUMSS

Selecting the HUMSS (Humanities and Social Sciences) strand provides opportunities in the fields of humanities, social sciences, communication, and more. Common courses include literature, philosophy, sociology, psychology, and political science. HUMSS programs focus on developing critical thinking, communication, and analytical skills, preparing students for careers in journalism, social work, public relations, teaching, and other humanities-related fields. Graduates may pursue degrees in fields such as literature, social sciences, communication, or education, leading to diverse career paths in research, writing, advocacy, and education..
