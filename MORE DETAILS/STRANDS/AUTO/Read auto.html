What is Automotive Strand?

The AUTOMOTIVE strand is a specialization within the Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) track in senior high school. This strand is designed for students who are interested in pursuing a career in the automotive industry. It focuses on providing practical skills and knowledge related to automotive technology, including areas such as automotive servicing, maintenance, repair, and diagnostics.

Why Automotive?

Selecting the Automotive strand within the TVL track provides opportunities in the automotive industry, emphasizing skills related to vehicle maintenance, repair, and technology. Common courses include automotive technology, engine maintenance, and electrical systems. Graduates from this strand can pursue careers as automotive technicians, mechanics, or service advisors in automotive repair shops, dealerships, or even start their own automotive businesses. The program typically includes hands-on training, preparing students for real-world scenarios. Additionally, further education or certifications in specialized areas within automotive technology can open doors to advanced roles and opportunities in the evolving automotive field.
